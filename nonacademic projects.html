Project Dariya
Enactus SGGSCC

• Designed a dual-pronged business model to address India's escalating waste crisis : o First segment : Featured the deployment of an innovative device known as Wastrawl which was strategically positioned in stagnant water bodies to retrieve improperly disposed waste. o Second segment: Introduced a waste bank initiative known as PLEX, concentrating on establishing waste exchange systems within urban areas. This segment incentivized the collection of dry waste by offering rewards such as cash, food, and clothing to marginalized communities like domestic workers and local scavengers. o Revenue: Generated by selling the collected waste from both segments to third-party recyclers. • Employed 28 entrepreneurs from these communities across both segments and achieved a revenue exceeding INR 650,000. • Won the Race to Rethink Plastic at the Enactus World Cup 2021 and secured a prize of USD 20,000.

Project Kashti
Enactus SGGSCC

• Devised a comprehensive business model aimed at transforming rural communities into smart villages while concurrently fostering local employment opportunities. • Implemented a six-segmented strategy encompassing: o Skill Development: Provided tailored vocational training to villagers, enabling them to achieve financial independence. o Water Filtration: Established RO plants to address high TDS levels and distributed portable water filters at subsidized rates, thereby ensuring access to purified drinking water while creating employment opportunities for distributors. o Waste Management: Equipped households with dual dustbins for effective waste segregation, with regular collection and recycling facilitated through a dedicated plant, benefiting farmers with access to affordable manure. o Sanitation: Introduced a novel liquid hand-wash manufacturing process managed by rural women, generating additional livelihood opportunities while promoting hygiene practices. o Smart Education: Established public libraries and cyber cafes, managed by local entrepreneurs, functioning as training hubs for technical skills acquisition at nominal fees. o Improved Agricultural Practices: Introduced an innovative Micro Sprinkler Irrigation System to optimize costs and enhance agricultural yield for local farmers. • Employed 204 villagers and generate over INR 1,000,000 in revenue.
